Summaeverythang


A speculative rebrand for Summaeverythang, a mutual aid organization serving South Central LA and Watts. Beautiful projects deserve beautiful identities and this one wasn't getting the visual representation it earned.

Problem

The existing identity felt like it had nothing to do with the organization. It didn't reflect the creativity and care Lauren Halsey puts into this program, or the community it exists for. It was polished in a way that felt like it was trying to look legitimate instead of just being it.

Design Intent

The visual language was already alive in the community, I felt that it wasn't necessary for me to fully invent one. Stickers, crates, spray paint, cardboard. The food supply chain became the backbone of the system, with the apple and banana as the identity's anchors two of the most nutrient-dense and universally recognized foods in the world, and an honest symbol of what this organization actually does for people.

The sticker label became my information holder, placed the way they actually show up on our produce: randomly, no forced grid. I didn't want it so polished it went sterile again or too avant-garde that valuable help was overlooked. Getting help and needing help should feel uncomplicated.

Process

Full scope: visual system, identity, concept, typography, copywriting, print production, and mockups (more experimental material applications than I'd done before). Billboards, posters, packaging, banners, bumper stickers, a van mockup, sticky notes and a physical cooler.

The real constraint was making cardboard, paper scraps, and airbrushing look intentional and professional in print and digital without killing what made them honest in the first place.

Airbrush Prototyping Visual Research Laser Cut Experimentation Material Research

Outcome

Presented at a class exhibition. This project taught me that community-facing design lives in the balance between professionalism, approachability, and style. Getting that balance right means getting to know the community you're designing for.
