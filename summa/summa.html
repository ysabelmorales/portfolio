Summaeverythang


Limited print Edition 1 of 3 held in the Otis College's library's Special Collections. A BFA thesis exploring my own religious deconstruction through print. Six handbound works created using coptic stitching, screenprinting and ASCII art overlays to examine our inherited faith and lived experiences. Each work takes inspiration from different formats: devotional book, voter pamphlet, recipe card etc...


Material research started at Kelly Paper and Hiromi Paper in Los Angeles. If I was going to take this publication thing seriously, I needed to see and feel every sheet. Weight, texture, and my color pallet came from the paper itself. Soft thick cardstock for the recipe card, lightweight textured rice paper for the voter pamphlet. ASCII art made sense for my photo treatments because of the tension it holds. I had found community in digital spaces, but liturgical text made me feel unwelcome, so the contrast felt inevitable. It also makes you physically hold it back to see the bigger picture, which felt right as well.
